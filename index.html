Indeterminacy

Indeterminacy is a composing approach in which some aspects of a work are left open to chance or to the interpreter’s free choice. John Cage, a pioneer of indeterminacy, defined it as “the ability of a piece to be performed in substantially different ways.”

For our series on indeterminacy, we invite you to create and share your own posters with this tool.
